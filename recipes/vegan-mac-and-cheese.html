Vegan Mac-and-Cheese

This vegan mac-and-cheese is a creamy, cheesy delight made with cashews and nutritional yeast. It's perfect for a comforting meal or a quick weeknight dinner.

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Total Time: 35 minutes

Servings: 4

Calories: 350 per serving

Difficulty: Easy

Dietary Restrictions: Vegan, Gluten-Free

Allergens: Nuts

Equipment: Blender, Pot, Oven (optional)

Notes: For a spicier version, add 3 tablespoons of tobasco hot sauce to the cheese sauce before mixing with the pasta.

Variations: Add steamed vegetables like broccoli or spinach for extra nutrition. For a smoky flavor, add smoked paprika to the cheese sauce.

Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop.

Freezing: This dish can be frozen for up to 2 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Pairings: Serve with a side salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.

Substitutions: Use any non-dairy milk instead of almond milk. Swap cashews with sunflower seeds for a nut-free version.

Tips: Soak the cashews in water for at least 2 hours before blending for a creamier texture. Adjust the thickness of the cheese sauce by adding more or less almond milk.

Serving Suggestions: Top with fresh herbs like parsley or chives for added flavor. Serve with a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for a kick.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cashews
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli (optional)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons of tobasco hot sauce

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine the cashews, nutritional yeast, almond milk, lemon juice, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. In a large pot, combine the cooked pasta and the cashew cheese sauce. Stir until the pasta is well coated.
  4. If using, add the steamed broccoli and mix well.
  5. For a crunchy topping, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a small bowl, mix the breadcrumbs with olive oil and paprika. Sprinkle over the mac-and-cheese.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown.
